3-Day Syllabus

From understanding yourself, to understanding others,
to understanding the world you live in.

1

Being here

We begin by naming what we actually seek.

The human being

Our dimensions of living — intellectual, behavioural, occupational, and what lies beyond the physical.

Mind & body

The distinction between mind and body — explored through direct observation and reflective exercises.

The mind's faculties

Reason, emotion, will — how they function, when they fall short, and what restores them.

"Understanding the mind is the key to understanding everything else."

2

Relationships & Family

Why relationships work and what is at the root

Marriage & purpose

Is marriage arbitrary, or does it carry real meaning? What holds it together over a lifetime?

Family life

Responsibilities and expectations. Being a parent, a child, a sibling, a friend — what each role actually demands.

Emotional life

Trust, affection, love — their actual nature, and how the mind shapes every relationship we have.

"A deeper look at the core of all human interaction."

3

Society, nature & reality

We examine education, production, inequality, conflict.

The ultimate human goal

What is the deepest aspiration of the human race? And how close are we to understanding it?

Nature & existence

The fundamental building blocks of nature. Evolution, cosmic design, and our place within it.

Summary & way forward

Mind, relationships, family, society, nature — tied into a coherent whole. What comes next for each participant.

"Connecting all aspects of life into one continuous whole."

What is this Workshop based on?

The Sah Astitva Answers Workshop is rooted in Jeevan Vidya — the universalisation wing of Madhyasth Darshan, a new discovery that transcends existing frameworks of human knowledge. Propounded by Shri A. Nagraj.

It provides a grand unified view of existence: self, family, society, and nature as a single coherent whole.

The philosophy offers a universal resolution to the questions we carry as individuals — and to the larger contradictions of our time: ecological, social, personal, and existential.
