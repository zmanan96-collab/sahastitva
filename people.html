The people who share this.

We are humans from diverse walks of life — driven by an honest quest to understand reality and live with meaning and harmony on this planet.

We are engineers, farmers, architects, homemakers, scientists, social activists, corporate professionals, monks, educators, and students. Rich, middle-class, and poor. From villages, towns, and cities across India.

We have no religious or political affiliation, no formal membership, and no hierarchy. What binds us is a common vision and a shared resolution — found in this framework.

Workshops are not a source of livelihood. We are engaged in various activities for our material needs. The teaching is shared because we believe it is the fundamental need of the human being — and the long-term solution to humanity's problems.

Those engaged seriously with the philosophy number in the few hundreds. Some have come together at dedicated centres across India for study, practice, and dissemination. Not everyone who studies also conducts workshops.

The list below is representative. Names and pictures are intentionally withheld — the teaching is what matters. That said, the teaching resides in the teacher. Words are only a medium.

Anil Joshi

Jeevan Vidya Practitioner & Workshop Facilitator

Anil didn't come to Jeevan Vidya through a guru or a recommendation. He came through exhaustion. After a decade of professional success and personal confusion — a marriage that had patterns he couldn't break, a career that rewarded him but didn't satisfy him — he sat down one day and asked himself the most honest question he'd ever asked: "Is this it?"

A friend introduced him to Madhyasth Darshan. He left with the feeling that someone had finally answered a question he'd been carrying for ten years. He spent the next two years studying it seriously — not as a profession, but as a human being who had found something real.

He facilitates not as a teacher who has transcended ordinary life, but as a person who has understood it more clearly — and who finds the deepest meaning in watching others do the same.

"I'm not here to give you answers. I'm here to help you find the questions clearly enough that the answers become obvious. That's what Jeevan Vidya did for me — and that's what I try to do in the room."

— Anil Joshi
