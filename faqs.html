A 3-day residential workshop where you examine your own life through dialogue. Not a lecture. Not a retreat. A structured conversation — about who you are, how you live, and what you actually want.

No preaching. No rituals. No texts to follow.

To arrive at a clear, integral understanding of yourself — your mind, your relationships, your place in the world.

Not as a concept. As something you can actually live.

The workshop moves from the inside out:

Mind and consciousness → Relationships and family → Society and human purpose → Nature and reality.

Every topic connects to every other. By the end, the pieces form a coherent whole.

Jeevan Vidya is an introduction to Madhyasth Darshan (Coexistential Philosophy), a framework developed by Shri A. Nagraj.

It does not ask you to adopt this philosophy. It asks you to examine it — and draw your own conclusions.

No teaching fee. Ever.

A nominal daily contribution of ₹300–600 covers food and basic accommodation for residential participants. If this is a barrier, speak with the organizers — contribution is never a condition for attending.

At designated locations across India. Specific venues and dates are listed on the Schedule page.

Simple shared accommodation on campus. Basic bedding is provided — bring an extra bedsheet or blanket if possible. Rooms are allotted based on availability.

Nutritious vegetarian meals are provided throughout. Jain and dairy-free preferences can be accommodated — check with organizers in advance. Children below 10 are not charged for food.

Classroom sessions run from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, with breaks for meals and tea. An evening group dialogue is held from 8:00–9:00 PM after dinner.

Local driver contacts are shared with registered participants. Join the relevant WhatsApp group or reach out to the organizers for travel details.

The workshop is designed as a complete journey — each day builds on the one before. Partial attendance is possible, but it limits the understanding the workshop is meant to give. Full attendance is strongly recommended.

No alcohol, tobacco, or intoxicants. Participants are advised not to bring valuables. A simple lifestyle is expected throughout.

Beginners' reading material is shared. Advanced workshops and a study guide are available for those who want to continue. Many participants stay connected with facilitators and local groups for ongoing study.

Anyone willing to think — honestly and independently.

Students, professionals, homemakers, educators, thinkers, parents, social workers. No prior knowledge needed. No belief system required.
