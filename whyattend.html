What participants take back.

The framework speaks to every stage and situation of life.

Making sense of your:

  • — Studies and career
  • — Peers and surroundings
  • — Relationships — with parents, siblings and friends
  • — Your aspirations, true capabilities, identity
  • — Your role in the 'larger scheme' of things
  • — 'Balancing' your aspirations, work and family responsibilities
  • — Understanding yourself and your state of mind
  • — Relating to your children, spouses and friends
  • — Exploring your 'true-purpose' of life
  • — Environmental activism — the basic laws of nature and how we can live with complementarity
  • — Social Activism: evolutionary and societal reasons for inequality and how they can be resolved
  • — How the human race is one
  • — Go beyond 'cause and effect' to understand the formless dimensions of reality
  • — Understand the 'observer' in the 'observation'
  • — Understand existence as reality and man's place in it
  • — The true nature of the 'spirit' in a non-mystical, realisable manner
  • — Resolve the mind-body problem
  • — The nature of 'consciousness' and its many activities and its relationship with the brain
  • — Understand human behaviour and its actual basis and reasons
  • — Role of material needs and its relationship with prosperity and the dependency on nature
  • — Role of beliefs and conditioning in our perspective of reality
  • — How stones, plants and animals also have religion. How human religion is one.
